The velocity estimator:

The velocity estimator:
$$\sigma_\hat{v} = -\frac{\ln(10)c}{5}\left(\frac{(1+z)c}{H(z)r(z)} - 1\right)^{-1}\sigma_\mu$$

The Maximum-Likelihood method

We can approximate the velocity field as a Gaussian random field

\[ \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{p}_{\rm HD}) = (2 \pi)^{-\frac{n}{2}}|C(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{p}_{\rm HD})|^{-\frac{1}{2}} \times\exp\left[-\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{\hat{v}}^T(\mathbf{p}_{\rm HD})C(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{p}_{\rm HD})^{-1}\mathbf{\hat{v}}(\mathbf{p}_{\rm HD}) \right] \]

\(\mathbf{p}_\mathrm{HD} = \left\{M_0, \alpha, \beta, \sigma_M\right\}\) are the SNe Ia Hubble diagram parameters


\(\mathbf{p} = \left\{f\sigma_8, \sigma_v, \sigma_u\right\}\) are growth-rate related parameters


\(C_{ij}(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{p}_\mathrm{HD}) = C_{ij}^{vv}(f\sigma_8, \sigma_u) + (\sigma_v^2 + \sigma_\hat{v}^2(\mathbf{p}_{HD}))\delta_{ij}\) the covariance matrix

$C_{ij}^{vv} \propto (f\sigma_8)^2\int_0^{+\infty}{P_{\theta\theta}(k) W_{ij}(k)\mathrm{sinc}^2(\sigma_u k) dk}$

The selection bias

The sample bias appears on Hubble diagram residuals at \(z \sim 0.06\) and grows up to \(\Delta\mu \sim -0.13\) at \(z \sim 0.12\)

It results into a positive bias on estimated peculiar velocities



Only the estimated velocities are biased !!!

Results for a ZTF 6-years complete sample

We can build a bias free sample by applying a redshift cut at \(z = 0.06\)



Results for our 27 mocks :


  • Fit with $v_\mathrm{true}$: \( \langle f\sigma_8 \rangle = 0.991 \pm 0.016 \), \(\sqrt{\left<\sigma_{f \sigma_8}^2\right>} = 0.100\)
  • Fit fixing \(\mathbf{p}_\mathrm{HD}\): \( \langle f\sigma_8 \rangle = 1.036 \pm 0.031 \), \(\sqrt{\left<\sigma_{f \sigma_8}^2\right>} = 0.185\)
  • Fit with $\mathbf{p}$, $\mathbf{p}_\mathrm{HD}$ free: \( \langle f\sigma_8 \rangle = 0.998 \pm 0.037 \), \(\sqrt{\left<\sigma_{f \sigma_8}^2\right>} = 0.188\)

Conclusion

We developed and run a full detailed simulation and analysis pipeline to measure growth rate of structure using ZTF SNe Ia observations


Our analysis shows that the spectroscopic selection causes a bias for $f\sigma_8$ when using SNe Ia at $z>0.06$


We forecast that a 6-years ZTF SNe Ia spectro-identified sample cut at $z=0.06$ will produce a $f\sigma_8$ measurement with a precision of $\sim 19\%$ using velocities only


Correcting the bias does not improve much the constraint on $f\sigma_8$ but significant improvements are expected from future work on photometric typing analysis and combination with RSD
